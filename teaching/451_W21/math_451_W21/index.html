MATH 451: Numerical Analysis I (Sections 1 & 2)

Welcome to the course webpage for Math 451! This course is the first part of the Numerical Analysis sequence (Math 451-Math 452-Math 453) covering foundational numerical methods/algorithms used for problems that arise in many scientific fields. In particular, we will discuss upon the accuracy of numerical methods, their stability and efficiency will be considered in this course. Furthermore, we will discuss about implementations of the underlying algorithms in MATLAB, where the latter will be taught through increasingly complex codes over the quarter. In this web page and associated links, you will find important information about Math 451 including (but not limited to) syllabus, homework problems, MATLAB tutorials and important dates, among others.

Main Textbook: Elementary Numerical Analysis, Authors: Kendall Atkinson and Weimin Han, Publisher: John Wiley & Sons, 2004.

Class Material by Topic:

  • Taylor Polynomials, Finite Precision Arithmetic and Error Propagation (briefly)
  • Root-finding methods and Fixed-Point Theory
  • Interpolation and Approximation
  • Numerical Integration and Differentiation
  • Linear Equations (Direct and Iterative methods)
  • Advanced Topics in Numerical Linear Algebra
  • Introduction to Numerical methods for IVPs (if time permits)
Syllabus and Tentative Schedule:

Please click here for the complete course syllabus. Make sure you make copies of the syllabus bring it with you in class. A tentative schedule is as follows:

  • Week of 2/15: Numerical Differentiation (Section 5.4) and Linear Equations I (read Sections 6.1-6.2).
  • Week of 2/22: Linear Equations II (Sections 6.3-6.5).
  • Week of 3/1: Linear Equations III (Section 6.6) including steepest descent and conjugate gradient methods.
  • Week of 3/8: Systems of Nonlinear Equations (Section 7.3); Eigenvalue problem (Section 7.2); Least Squares Fitting (Section 7.1) if time permits.
