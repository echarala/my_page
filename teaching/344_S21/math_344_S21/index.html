MATH 344: Linear Analysis II (Section 1)

Welcome to the course webpage for Math 344! This course is the second part of the Linear Analysis sequence (Math 244-Math 344) covering foundational methods and techniques primarily used for (linear) ordinary differential equations (ODEs) and partial differential equations (PDEs). In particular, we will discuss upon the Laplace transform, power series methods, Fourier series techniques as well as orthogonality of functions and Sturm-Liouville theory. In this web page and associated links, you will find important information about Math 344 including (but not limited to) syllabus, homework problems, and important dates, among others.

Syllabus and Tentative Schedule:

Please click here for the complete course syllabus. Make sure you make copies of the syllabus bring it with you in class. A very tentative schedule is as follows:

  • Week of 3/29: Laplace Transform Part I (Properties, inverse transform and IVPs).
  • Week of 4/5: Laplace Transform Part II (Shifting theorems; convolution).
  • Week of 4/12: Series solutions of ODEs Part I (review of series; applications).
  • Week of 4/19: Series solutions of ODEs Part II (Regular singular points and Frobenius series).
  • Week of 4/26: Projections and orthogonal functions Part I (Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization).
  • Week of 5/3: Projections and orthogonal functions Part II (Normal equation and least-squares solution).
  • Week of 5/10: Fourier Series Part I (Definitions, theorems and properties).
  • Week of 5/17: Fourier Series Part II (Heat equation and BVPs).
  • Week of 5/24: Fourier Series Part III (Sturm-Liouville BVPs and special functions).
  • Week of 5/31: Fourier Series Part IV (cont'ed: Special functions).
